Familial adenomatous polyposis (FAP) is an autosomal dominant syndrome caused by a germline mutation in the adenomatous polyposis coli (APC) gene, characterized by numerous colorectal adenomas. In addition, FAP patients may develop extraintestinal manifestations. Several cases of hepatocellular adenomas (HCA) detected accidentally in FAP patients have raised the so-far unsolved question of whether they represent a specific manifestation of FAP or a mere coincidence.
