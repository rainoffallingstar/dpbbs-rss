The disability of migraine, a highly prevalent condition, is worsened by a second comorbid chronic pain condition. There is evidence of a relationship between migraine and several visceral pain conditions including irritable bowel syndrome, endometriosis, and dysmenorrhoea, as well as nonvisceral conditions including temporomandibular dysfunction, fibromyalgia, and lower back pain.
