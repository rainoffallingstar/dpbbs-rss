Streptococcus pneumoniae is a major human pathogen with a high burden of disease. Non-invasive isolates (those found in non-sterile sites) are thought to be a key source of invasive isolates (those found in sterile sites) and a reservoir of anti-microbial resistance (AMR) determinants. Despite this, pneumococcal surveillance has almost exclusively focused on invasive isolates.
