Electroanatomical mapping is a method for creating a model of the electrophysiology of the human heart. Medical professionals routinely locate and ablate the site of origin of cardiac arrhythmias with invasive catheterization. Non-invasive localization takes the form of electrocardiographic (ECG) or magnetocardiographic (MCG) imaging, where the goal is to reconstruct the electrical activity of the human heart.
