Disruption of protein-protein interactions is medicinally important. Interface helices may be mimicked in helical probes featuring enhanced rigidities, binding to protein targets, stabilities in serum, and cell uptake. This form of mimicry is dominated by stapling between side chains of helical residues: there has been less progress on helical N-caps, and there were no generalizable C-caps.
