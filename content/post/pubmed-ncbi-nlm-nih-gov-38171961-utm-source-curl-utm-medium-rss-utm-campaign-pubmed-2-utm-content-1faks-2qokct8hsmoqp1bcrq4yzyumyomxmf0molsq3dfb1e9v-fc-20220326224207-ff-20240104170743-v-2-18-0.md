We report the detection of a γ-ray bubble spanning at least 100deg² in ultra-high energy (UHE) up to a few PeV in the direction of the star-forming region Cygnus X, implying the presence super PeVatron(s) accelerating protons to at least 10 PeV. A log-parabola form with the photon index Γ(E)=(2.71±0.02)+(0.11±0.02)×log(10)(E/10TeV) is found fitting the gamma-ray energy spectrum of the bubble well.
