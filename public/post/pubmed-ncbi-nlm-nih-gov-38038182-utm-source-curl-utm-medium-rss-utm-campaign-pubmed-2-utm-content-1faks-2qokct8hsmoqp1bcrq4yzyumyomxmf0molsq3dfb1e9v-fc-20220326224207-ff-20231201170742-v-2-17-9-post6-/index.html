Here to stay? Policy changes in alcohol home delivery and "to-go" sales during and after COVID-19 in the United States

During the early phase of the COVID-19 pandemic, legislative changes that expanded alcohol home delivery and options for “to-go” alcohol sales were introduced across the United States to provide economic relief to establishments and retailers. Using data from the Alcohol Policy Information System, we examined whether these changes have persisted beyond the peak phase of the COVID-19 emergency and explored the implications for public health.
