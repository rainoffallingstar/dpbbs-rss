Neurofibromatosis 1 (NF1) mutation results in impaired function of human induced pluripotent stem cell-derived microglia

Neurofibromatosis type 1 (NF1) is an autosomal dominant condition caused by germline mutations in the NF1 gene. Children with NF1 are prone to the development of multiple nervous system abnormalities, including autism and brain tumors, which could reflect the effect of NF1 mutation on microglia function.
