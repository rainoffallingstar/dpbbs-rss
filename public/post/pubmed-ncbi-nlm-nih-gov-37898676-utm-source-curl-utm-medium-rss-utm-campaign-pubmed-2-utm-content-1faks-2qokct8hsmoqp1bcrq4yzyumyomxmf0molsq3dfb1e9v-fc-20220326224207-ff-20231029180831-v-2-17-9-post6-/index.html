Spatial correlation of 2D hard-tissue histology with 3D microCT scans through 3D printed phantoms

Hard-tissue histology-the analysis of thin two-dimensional (2D) sections-is hampered by the opaque nature of most biological specimens, especially bone. Therefore, the cutting process cannot be assigned to regions of interest. In addition, the applied cutting-grinding method is characterized by significant material loss.
