The 14th Amendment

Of all the amendments to the U.S. Constitution, the 14th is a big one. It’s shaped all of our lives, whether we realize it or not: Roe v. Wade, Brown v. Board of Education, Bush v. Gore, plus other Supreme Court cases that legalized same-sex marriage, interracial marriage, access to birth control — they’ve all been built on the back of the 14th. The amendment was ratified after the Civil War, and it’s packed full of lofty phrases like due process, equal protection, and liberty.
