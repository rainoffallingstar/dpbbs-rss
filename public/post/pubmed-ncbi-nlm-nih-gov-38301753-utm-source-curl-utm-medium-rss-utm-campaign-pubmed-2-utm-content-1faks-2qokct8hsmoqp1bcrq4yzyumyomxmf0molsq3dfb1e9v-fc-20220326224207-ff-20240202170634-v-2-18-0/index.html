Improving Reporting of Exercise Capacity Across Age Ranges Using Novel Workload Reference Equations

Exercise capacity (EC) is an important predictor of survival in the general population as well as in individuals with cardiopulmonary disease. Despite its relevance, considering percent-predicted workload (%pWL) given by current equations may overestimate EC in older adults. Therefore, to improve the reporting of EC in clinical practice, our main objective was to develop workload reference equations (pWL) that better reflect the relation between workload and age.
