By Accident of Birth (2022)

In August of 1895, a ship called the SS Coptic approached the coast of Northern California. On that boat was a passenger from San Francisco, a young man named Wong Kim Ark. He was returning home after visiting his wife and child in China, and he’d taken similar trips before. But when the ship docked, officials told him he couldn’t get off. The customs agent barred him according to the Chinese Exclusion Act, which denied citizenship to Chinese immigrants.
