The locations of stroke lesions next to the posterior internal capsule may predict the recovery of the related proprioceptive deficits

CONCLUSION: Lesions dorsal to internal capsule next to the posterior CST were associated with proprioceptive deficits and may have predictive value. Higher lesion load was correlated with poorer restoration of proprioceptive function. Furthermore, patients with SSEP loss trended towards poor recovery of proprioception, the corresponding lesions were also located in the same location.
