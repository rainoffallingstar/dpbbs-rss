Large-scale deorphanization of <em>Nematostella vectensis</em> neuropeptide G protein-coupled receptors supports the independent expansion of bilaterian and cnidarian peptidergic systems

Neuropeptides are ancient signaling molecules in animals but only few peptide receptors are known outside bilaterians. Cnidarians possess a large number of G protein-coupled receptors (GPCRs) - the most common receptors of bilaterian neuropeptides - but most of these remain orphan with no known ligands.
